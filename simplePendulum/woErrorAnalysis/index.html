Simple pendulum JavaScript integrator

This webpage uses the Runge-Kutta-Fehlberg fourth-order method with fifth-order error checking (RKF45) to approximate the solution to the problem of the simple pendulum: $$\dfrac{d^2 \theta}{dt^2} = -\dfrac{g}{l} \cos{\theta}$$ where \(g\) is the acceleration due to gravity in metres per second squared, \(l\) is the length of the pendulum in metres, \(\theta\) is the angle from the positive \(x\) axis (in radians) and \(t\) is the time in seconds. Below you can specify the various parameters for the problem we will solve. Integrating our ordinary differential equation with respect to \(\theta\), multiplying by two and square rooting yields: $$\dot{\theta} = \pm \sqrt{\dot{\theta}_0^2 + \dfrac{2g}{l}\left(\sin{\theta_0} - \sin{\theta}\right)}$$. From this equation, the period of the default problem is approximated, namely as: \[ \begin{aligned} T &= 2\int_{-\pi}^0 \dfrac{d\theta}{\sqrt{-19.62 \sin{\theta}}} \\ &\approx 2.367841947576237 \hspace{0.1cm}\mathrm{s}. \end{aligned} \]
Parameter Value Explanation
Acceleration due to gravity in \(\mathrm{m} \cdot \mathrm{s}^{-2}\).
Length of the pendulum rod in metres (m).
Starting time for the simulation in seconds (s).
End time for the simulation in seconds. The default \(t_f - t_0\) value is equal to four times the period \(T\) of the problem with the other default parameters.
Angle from the positive \(x\)-axis in radians at time \(t_0\).
First derivative of \(\theta\) with respect to \(t\) at \(t_0\) (in \(\mathrm{radians}\cdot \mathrm{s}^{-1}\)).
Error tolerance in both \(\theta\) and \(\dot{\theta}\). \(\epsilon \lt \)2e-12 often freezes the webpage.
